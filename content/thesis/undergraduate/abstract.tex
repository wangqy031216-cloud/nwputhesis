% 中文摘要
\begin{abstract}
大语言模型后训练的关键，是将人类偏好或高质量示范响应转化为可优化的奖励信号。无论显式训练奖励模型，还是通过直接偏好优化等方式隐式刻画偏好目标，其核心都在于为策略更新提供偏好导向的优化信号。现有方法在这一过程中仍面临多重问题：用来训练奖励信号的数据可能带有主观偏差和噪声；奖励信号可能随着策略分布变化而发生分布失配；若缺少对奖励更新幅度的限制，奖励尺度和语义容易漂移，进而诱发奖励过优化和训练不稳定的问题。

针对上述问题，本文提出近端逆奖励优化方法PIRO（Proximal Inverse Reward Optimization），面向大语言模型偏好对齐中的奖励函数学习问题，从高质量示范响应中恢复可用于策略优化的奖励信号，并在策略分布变化时保持其稳定性。PIRO在策略—奖励迭代更新的逆强化学习框架下学习奖励函数，以高质量示范响应作为偏好行为证据，减少对额外成对偏好比较数据的依赖，缓解偏好比较环节带来的标注噪声影响；同时，使奖励函数随当前策略分布动态更新，以减轻固定奖励信号与生成分布之间的失配；另一方面，通过引入相邻轮次奖励变化约束，有效抑制了奖励尺度膨胀和语义漂移。理论上，本文将对齐过程建模为奖励学习与策略优化耦合的双层问题，推导局部替代目标相对于原始对齐目标的改进下界，揭示奖励变化误差对原始目标稳定改进的影响。算法上，本文以相邻轮次奖励输出的均方根差（RMSD）近似奖励变化，实现对奖励变化幅度的可监测与自适应控制。

本文在TL;DR摘要生成和UltraFeedback通用指令跟随任务上进行验证。TL;DR任务中，PIRO在第五轮迭代中取得奖励评分$1.415$，较固定奖励RLHF/PPO提升约$14.1\%$，较SFT提升约$30.0\%$；去除奖励变化约束后，模型早期短暂上升，随后持续退化，说明该约束对维持多轮 IRL 训练稳定性具有关键作用。UltraFeedback任务中，PIRO最佳检查点MT-Bench Avg(8)为$7.53$，超过SFT、DPO、RLHF(PPO)、RLHF(GRPO)以及无约束IRL基线，并在推理和STEM等维度上取得提升。实验结果表明，PIRO 在动态奖励学习过程中能够同时提升策略性能与奖励稳定性。

\keywordslist{大语言模型对齐, 逆强化学习, 奖励模型, RMSD约束, 策略优化}
\end{abstract}

% 英文摘要
\begin{engabstract}
Post-training alignment of large language models needs reward signals that turn human preferences or high-quality demonstration responses into usable policy updates. In practice, this step is fragile. Preference supervision may carry subjective noise; a fixed reward signal can lose reliability as the policy distribution moves; and if reward updates are left unconstrained, the reward scale and meaning may drift, causing reward overoptimization and unstable training.

This thesis proposes Proximal Inverse Reward Optimization (PIRO), a reward-learning method for preference alignment from high-quality demonstration responses. PIRO learns the reward function in an inverse reinforcement learning loop with alternating reward learning and policy optimization. The high-quality demonstration responses serve as behavioral evidence of preference, which reduces dependence on extra pairwise labels. Samples from the current policy let the reward function track the changing generation distribution. A proximal constraint then limits reward changes between adjacent iterations. On the theory side, the alignment process is written as a bilevel problem that couples reward learning with KL-regularized policy optimization, and an improvement lower bound shows how reward-change error affects the original objective. In implementation, PIRO uses the root mean square difference (RMSD) between adjacent reward outputs as a computable proxy for reward change and adjusts the constraint coefficient during training.

Experiments on TL;DR summarization and UltraFeedback instruction following support this design. On TL;DR, PIRO reaches a reward score of $1.415$ at the fifth iteration, about $14.1\%$ higher than fixed-reward RLHF/PPO and about $30.0\%$ higher than SFT; without the reward-change constraint, performance rises briefly and then keeps falling. On UltraFeedback, PIRO obtains an MT-Bench Avg(8) score of $7.53$, higher than SFT, DPO, RLHF(PPO), RLHF(GRPO), and unconstrained IRL, with gains in reasoning and STEM. These results suggest that PIRO improves policy quality while keeping dynamic reward learning stable.

\engkeywordslist{LLM alignment, inverse reinforcement learning, reward model, RMSD constraint, policy optimization}
\end{engabstract}

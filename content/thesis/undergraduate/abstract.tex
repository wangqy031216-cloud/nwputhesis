\begin{abstract}
大语言模型后训练的关键，是将人类偏好或专家行为转化为可优化的奖励信号。无论显式训练奖励模型，还是通过直接偏好优化等方式学习隐式奖励函数，其核心都在于用奖励函数引导策略更新。现有方法在这一过程中仍面临多重问题：偏好数据和人类标注可能引入主观偏差；奖励信号可能随策略分布变化而逐渐错位；若缺少对奖励更新幅度的限制，奖励尺度和语义容易漂移，进而诱发奖励过优化和训练不稳定。

针对上述问题，本文研究大语言模型偏好对齐中的奖励函数学习，目标是从专家示范数据中恢复奖励信号，并在策略分布变化时保持稳定。本文提出近端逆奖励优化方法PIRO（Proximal Inverse Reward Optimization），以专家示范响应作为偏好行为证据，在策略 - 奖励迭代更新的逆强化学习框架下直接从示范数据中学习奖励函数，减少对额外成对偏好标注的依赖，降低偏好比较环节可能引入的主观误差；同时，PIRO限制相邻轮次奖励变化，使奖励模型适应当前策略分布并避免无约束漂移。理论上，本文将对齐过程建模为奖励学习与策略优化耦合的双层问题，推导改进下界，说明奖励变化误差项会影响原始目标的稳定改进。工程实现上，本文以相邻轮次奖励输出的均方根差（RMSD）近似奖励变化，并用自适应约束系数形成可观测、可调节的稳定性反馈。

本文在TL;DR摘要生成和UltraFeedback通用指令跟随任务上进行验证。TL;DR任务中，PIRO在iter5取得奖励评分$1.415$，较固定奖励RLHF/PPO提升约$14.1\%$，较SFT提升约$30.0\%$；去除奖励变化约束后，模型早期短暂上升，随后持续退化，说明该约束对多轮IRL训练稳定性具有必要作用。UltraFeedback任务中，PIRO最佳检查点MT-Bench Avg(8)为$7.53$，超过SFT、DPO、RLHF(PPO)、RLHF(GRPO)以及无约束IRL基线，并在推理和STEM等维度上取得提升。实验表明，PIRO能够抑制动态奖励学习中的奖励漂移，提升多轮逆强化学习偏好对齐训练的稳定性。

\keywordslist{大语言模型对齐, 逆强化学习, 奖励模型, RMSD约束, 策略优化}
\end{abstract}

% 英文摘要
\begin{engabstract}
The key of post-training large language models is to turn human preferences or expert behavior into optimizable reward signals. Whether a method trains an explicit reward model or learns an implicit reward function through direct preference optimization, the reward signal guides policy updates. Existing methods still face several difficulties: preference data may introduce subjective bias, rewards may become misaligned with the changing policy distribution, and unconstrained reward updates may cause reward-scale inflation, semantic drift, overoptimization, and unstable training.

Motivated by this problem, this thesis studies reward-function learning for preference alignment of large language models. The goal is to recover a reward signal from expert demonstrations and keep it stable as the policy distribution changes. This thesis proposes Proximal Inverse Reward Optimization (PIRO). PIRO learns the reward function directly from expert demonstration responses in an inverse reinforcement learning framework, reducing reliance on additional pairwise preference labels and their annotation error. It also bounds reward changes between adjacent iterations, allowing the reward model to adapt to the current policy while avoiding unconstrained drift. The analysis formulates alignment as a bilevel problem and derives an improvement lower bound showing how reward-change errors affect the original objective. The algorithm uses the root mean square difference (RMSD) between reward outputs and an adaptive coefficient as an observable stability signal.

The method is evaluated on TL;DR summarization with Pythia-1B and UltraFeedback instruction following with Qwen3.5-9B. On TL;DR, PIRO reaches $1.415 \pm 0.054$ at iter5, $14.1\%$ above RLHF/PPO and $30.0\%$ above SFT. Removing the $C\epsilon$ constraint gives an early gain followed by degradation, indicating the necessity of constraining reward changes. On UltraFeedback, PIRO's best checkpoint scores $7.53$ on MT-Bench Avg(8), outperforming SFT, DPO, RLHF(PPO), RLHF(GRPO), and the unconstrained IRL baseline, with clearer gains on reasoning and STEM. These results indicate that PIRO suppresses reward drift and improves multi-round IRL-based preference alignment.

\engkeywordslist{LLM alignment, IRL, reward model, RMSD constraint}
\end{engabstract}
